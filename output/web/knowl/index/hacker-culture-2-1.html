The term hacker comes from the sound that programmers would make when typing or hacking away at keyboards. Originally a hacker was anyone who worked feverishly at a problem on a computer and the term cracker was used to define people who attempted to bypass security systems. The distinction was eventually lost and a hacker has come to be the popular term for someone attempting to gain unauthorized access to data. For an interesting glimpse into early hacker culture/reasoning read The Conscience of a Hacker
